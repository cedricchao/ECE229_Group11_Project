User Story

Majority of potential users for this recommendation system are students. While designing products, product managers need to look at things from the perspective of potential users. A potential end-user could be a second-year data science major student who is on the path of finishing his major requirements and also the general education (GE) requirements. In addition, he might be struggling to improve his GPA because he did not do very well in the first year. Besides his academic interests, he is also interested in taking courses purely out of interest, like music and theatre classes. Besides school, he has extracurricular activities and clubs to attend to. He needs to find a balance between school and extracurricular. In summary, his needs are finishing major/GE requirements, improving his GPA, and discovering potential interests. For the first need, the product will have the information on the requirements of the data science major and his progress so that the product will not recommend courses that he has already taken. For the second need, the product will first ask him to input his current GPA and then list the average hours dedicated and previous GPA of the recommended courses so that he can decide for himself. For the last need, the product will list classes that others spoke highly of and also have nice GPAs so that he can keep up with both his interests and GPA improvement plan.
